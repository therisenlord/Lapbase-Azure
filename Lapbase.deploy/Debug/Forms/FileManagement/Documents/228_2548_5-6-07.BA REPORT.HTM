BARIUM SWALLOW:

Swallow examination was performed, targetted to the gastro-oesophageal
junction.

The area of gastric banding appears to be orientated appropriately and
there is no portion of the gastric lumen demonstrated proximal to the
site of banding.

The distal oesophagus is of normal calibre.

Moderate hold-up of contrast flow was demonstrated immediately proximal to
the banding with the patient in the upright position with eventual
partial clearing of the distal oesophagus but some residual barium
persisting.

With the patient positioned supine, head down and increasing
intra-abdominal pressure, no gastro-oesophageal reflux could be
demonstrated. However, swallowing in a non-dependant position showed
poor propulsion of barium through the oesophagus.
